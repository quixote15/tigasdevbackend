## Body Horror Fusion — CONCEPT REFERENCES

### The Thing (1982) — John Carpenter
- **Search**: "The Thing 1982 transformation scene" / "Rob Bottin practical effects"
- **Description**: THE definitive body horror fusion reference. Two organisms forced into one grotesque form. Study the Blair-Thing, the Norris-Thing, and the kennel scene for how two bodies become one with exposed muscle, bone, and asymmetric features.
- **Relevant elements**: Flesh merging, asymmetric fusion, exposed anatomy, the horror of losing individual identity.
- **Priority**: HIGH — direct inspiration for the fusion/split sequences.

### Tetsuo: The Iron Man (1989)
- **Search**: "Tetsuo Iron Man transformation"
- **Description**: Japanese body horror where flesh and metal fuse uncontrollably. The UNWILLING nature of the transformation and the grotesque aesthetic are directly relevant.
- **Relevant elements**: Involuntary fusion, machine/flesh hybrid (analogous to politics/flesh), visual distortion, black and white extreme contrast.
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — tonal reference.

### Akira (1988) — Tetsuo's Transformation
- **Search**: "Akira Tetsuo mutation scene"
- **Description**: The iconic manga/anime mutation scene. Flesh expanding, body parts growing uncontrollably. Reference for the 4-arm growth during fusion sequence.
- **Relevant elements**: Limb growth, body expansion, loss of control over one's form, grotesque but still character-recognizable.
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — limb growth animation reference.

### Basket Case (1982)
- **Search**: "Basket Case 1982 Belial"
- **Description**: B-movie about conjoined twins separated — one is a deformed mass. The low-budget grotesque aesthetic matches our Andre Guedes/B-movie style perfectly.
- **Relevant elements**: Deformed conjoined entity, practical effects grotesquery, B-movie charm, body horror comedy.
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — tonal match (body horror + comedy).

### Society (1989) — "Shunting" Scene
- **Search**: "Society 1989 shunting scene"
- **Description**: Bodies merging into a grotesque mass of flesh. The political metaphor (the elite literally absorbing others) is directly relevant to BolsoLula's theme.
- **Relevant elements**: Multiple bodies fusing, political commentary through body horror, grotesque comedy.
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — thematic parallel.

## Notes on Reference Usage

1. **Andre Guedes is the ONLY art style reference.** All other references inform CONTENT (what to draw), not STYLE (how to draw it).
2. Body horror references inform the FUSION SEQUENCE only — the general gameplay look is comic, not horror.
3. Political references inform accuracy of caricature features — which features to exaggerate and how.
4. Pixel art references inform technical implementation — how to achieve effects at 96x96 resolution.
5. **Never copy directly.** All references are for INSPIRATION. The final art must be original and match the Andre Guedes grotesque caricature style.
6. When in doubt about visual choices, default to: "What would Andre Guedes draw?" The answer is always: uglier, more exaggerated, more grotesque, funnier.
